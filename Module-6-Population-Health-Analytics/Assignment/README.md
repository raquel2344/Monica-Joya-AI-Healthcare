# Module 6 – Exploring Non-Traditional Health Data: Social Determinants of Health

## Purpose
I explored how social determinants of health (SDOH), the conditions outside of clinical settings that shape a person's well-being, can be collected and analyzed using AI tools. My paper examined three key areas: economic stability, education access, and neighborhood environment, along with the ethical challenges that come with using this type of data.

## Key Takeaways
I was struck by the statistic that social and behavioral factors account for as much as 60% of early deaths, while clinical care alone shapes only a fraction of overall health outcomes. AI and NLP tools can scan physician notes to detect unmet social needs like housing instability or food insecurity, and at the population level, AI models can layer SDOH data on top of disease tracking to anticipate where chronic illness is most likely to emerge.

## Insights
This assignment changed how I think about what healthcare data actually means. The most important factors in a patient's health often have nothing to do with what happens inside a clinic. But collecting SDOH data comes with real risks, including algorithmic bias and the potential to reinforce harmful stereotypes about vulnerable communities. As a mother, this hit close to home for me. Getting the technology right is only half the challenge.
