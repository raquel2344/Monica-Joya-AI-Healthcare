# Module 6 – Exploring Non-Traditional Health Data: Social Determinants of Health

## Purpose
This assignment explored how social determinants of health (SDOH), the conditions outside of clinical settings that shape a person's well-being, can be collected and analyzed using AI tools. The paper examined three key SDOH categories (economic stability, education access, and neighborhood environment) and discussed both the opportunities and ethical challenges of using this data in healthcare.

## Key Takeaways
Social and behavioral factors are responsible for as much as 60% of early deaths, while clinical care alone shapes only a fraction of overall health outcomes. AI and natural language processing tools can scan free-text physician notes to detect unmet social needs like unstable housing or food insecurity, helping care teams connect patients to the right support services. At the population level, AI models can layer SDOH data on top of disease tracking to anticipate where chronic illness is most likely to surface.

## Insights
This assignment changed how I think about what healthcare data actually means. The most important factors in a patient's health often have nothing to do with what happens inside a clinic. However, collecting SDOH data comes with real ethical risks, including algorithmic bias, patient privacy concerns, and the potential to reinforce harmful stereotypes about vulnerable communities. Getting the technology right is only half the challenge.
