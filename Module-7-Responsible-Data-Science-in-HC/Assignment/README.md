# Module 7 – Leveraging AI Technology in a Medical Case Study

## Purpose
This assignment analyzed a peer-reviewed case report involving a 55-year-old male with poorly controlled type 2 diabetes who developed an acute diabetic foot attack. The task was to identify three major health risks in the case and discuss how AI tools could help detect and manage those risks earlier.

## Key Takeaways
The three health risks identified were severe foot infection leading to sepsis, limb amputation, and cardiovascular deterioration. For each risk, there are AI tools already in development or available that could help: AI-assisted wound imaging for early detection of tissue damage, predictive sepsis models that flag deterioration before it becomes critical, and continuous glucose monitors with smart alerts to prevent the prolonged hyperglycemia that weakens immune response.

## Insights
The concept of "time is tissue" stood out in this assignment. In diabetic foot cases, delays in intervention sharply increase the risk of permanent limb loss. AI does not replace the clinician's judgment, but it can compress the time between a warning sign and a clinical response. This case study made the real-world stakes of AI in healthcare feel very concrete.
