# Module 8 – NLP in Healthcare: A Critical Review

## Purpose
This assignment critically reviewed how natural language processing (NLP) is being applied in healthcare, focusing on its use in extracting patient information from electronic health records (EHRs). The paper examined two key benefits and two limitations, along with ethical concerns around privacy, accuracy, and algorithmic bias.

## Key Takeaways
Roughly 80% of all healthcare documentation exists as free-text clinical notes, which are largely inaccessible to traditional computer-based analysis without NLP. NLP tools significantly reduce the administrative burden on clinicians (a documented driver of burnout and early retirement) and improve clinical decision-making by identifying conditions that may have been missed or recorded incorrectly. However, NLP models can produce errors when interpreting conversational language in clinical notes, and patient privacy remains a primary risk when processing sensitive health information at scale.

## Insights
This assignment highlighted a tension that runs through most AI applications in healthcare: the technology works best when it has access to large amounts of sensitive data, but that access introduces real risks. NLP has clear potential to make healthcare more efficient and more accurate, but it needs to be paired with strong privacy protections and continuous human oversight to avoid doing more harm than good.
