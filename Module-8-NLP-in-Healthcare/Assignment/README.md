# Module 8 – NLP in Healthcare: A Critical Review

## Purpose
I critically reviewed how natural language processing (NLP) is being applied in healthcare, focusing on its use in extracting patient information from electronic health records (EHRs). My paper examined two key benefits and two limitations, along with ethical concerns around privacy, accuracy, and algorithmic bias.

## Key Takeaways
I learned that roughly 80% of all healthcare documentation exists as free-text clinical notes, which are largely inaccessible to computer-based analysis without NLP. NLP tools can significantly reduce clinician burnout by cutting down on administrative work, and they improve decision-making by catching conditions that may have been missed or incorrectly recorded. At the same time, NLP models can misinterpret conversational language in clinical notes, and processing sensitive health data at scale introduces serious privacy risks.

## Insights
This assignment highlighted a tension I keep seeing across AI in healthcare: the technology works best when it has access to large amounts of sensitive data, but that access creates real vulnerabilities. NLP has clear potential to make healthcare more efficient and accurate, but without strong privacy protections and continuous human oversight, the risks can outweigh the benefits.
